Tips for Google searching

1. Selecting and refining keywords:

Use precise terms:

Instead of general phrases, search for specific keywords like "headache" instead of "my head hurts".

Modify your searches:

If your initial search is too broad, try adding more descriptive words or using different keywords.

Consider synonyms:

To include synonyms in your search, use a tilde (~) before a word.

Don't worry about spelling:

Google spell check will usually use the most common spelling of a word.

2. Advanced search operators:

Quotation marks (" "): Use quotation marks to search for an exact phrase.

Asterisk (*): Use an asterisk to replace an unknown word in a phrase.

Minus sign (-): Exclude words from search results.

Tilde (~): Include synonyms in your search.

Site: (site:): Search within a specific website.

Filetype: (filetype:): Search for specific file types.

Define: (define:): Find the definition of a word.

intitle: (intitle:): Search for pages with specific words in the title.

allintitle: (allintitle:): Search only for pages with a specific term in the title.

3. Using Google features:

Reverse Image Search: Search Google Images and use the camera button to find the origin of the image.

Unit conversion: Google can convert between different units of measurement.

Perform calculations: Perform calculations directly in the search bar.

Find weather: Search for "weather" to find the weather in your location or add a city name.

4. Other useful tips:

Using search suggestions: Google provides helpful suggestions as you type that can help you refine your search.

Consider the location of your search: If you're looking for a place or product, include location in your search.

Using voice search: Search by voice using the microphone icon.
