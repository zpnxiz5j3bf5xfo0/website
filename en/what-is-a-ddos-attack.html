What is a DDoS attack?

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is designed to force a website, computer, or online service offline. This is accomplished by flooding the target with many requests, consuming its capacity and rendering it unable to respond to legitimate requests. A DDoS attack differs from a Denial of Service (DoS) attack because it is distributed. The malicious traffic comes from a variety of different IP addresses, often the members of a botnet.

What is a botnet?
